Returning to Thailand with DTV Visa

Border Re-entry & 5-Year Long-Term Stay

Timeline Overview

November 19, 2025

Return to Thailand with DTV

  • Fly back to Chiang Mai with approved 5-year DTV visa
  • Show printed DTV approval at border
  • Stay for 180 days without re-entry permit
November 20, 2025 – February 1, 2026+

Stay in Thailand for 5+ Years

  • Leave and return every 180-360 days
  • Can stay continuously for up to 360 days with one extension
  • Extension costs approximately $300
  • Or do border run every 180 days for fresh entry

Border Re-Entry Procedures

What You'll Need

  • Approved DTV Visa Letter - Print multiple copies
  • Valid Passport - Must be valid for 6+ months
  • Return Flight Documentation - Proof of entry
  • Proof of Funds - Bank statements showing financial stability

At the Border

  1. Approach immigration with your passport and DTV approval letter
  2. Present the electronic approval (printed copy)
  3. Immigration will stamp your passport with the 5-year DTV visa
  4. Initial entry is valid for 180 days
  5. You can stay up to 360 days with an extension (costs ~$300)

Important Notes

⚠️ Key Points to Remember

  • No Re-entry Permits Needed - The DTV is valid for 5 years; you don't need to apply for re-entry permits for the next 5 years
  • 180-Day Entry - Your first entry stamp is valid for 180 days
  • Extension Option - You can extend for an additional 180 days (total 360 days) by paying ~$300
  • Border Runs - Alternatively, you can do a border run every 180 days (Laos, Myanmar, Malaysia) for a fresh entry
  • Continuous Stay - This visa allows you to stay continuously in Thailand for 5 years

Next Steps After Arrival

Immediate Actions (First Week)

  • Register your address with local immigration (TM.30)
  • Reconnect with your accommodation in Suthep
  • Verify your bank account still has sufficient funds
  • Schedule appointment with your primary care doctor
  • Attend recovery community meetings to re-establish support network

Longer-Term Considerations

  • Plan for annual border runs or extensions (every 180-360 days)
  • Keep medical treatment records updated for future visa applications
  • Consider visiting nearby countries during extension breaks
  • Budget for annual extension fees (~$300) if choosing that route
  • Explore opportunities for deeper community integration and activities

Financial Checklist

  • [ ] DTV application fee already paid ($340)
  • [ ] Extension fee reserved (~$300 for 180-day extension)
  • [ ] Border run costs calculated (if choosing that option)
  • [ ] Monthly living expenses budgeted for long-term stay
  • [ ] Financial Sponsor agreement still in effect

Travel Day & Arrival Guide

Complete flight logistics, border entry procedures, 60-day visa exemption process, and first-day setup in Chiang Mai.

Flight Details: BWI → CNX

Flight Route

Baltimore (BWI) → Detroit → Seoul → Chiang Mai (CNX)

Journey Duration

25+ hours total with layovers

Arrival Time

August 25, 2025 at 9:55 PM (Chiang Mai time)

Pre-Flight Preparation

  • □ Have passport ready and valid (minimum 6 months validity recommended)
  • □ Check airline baggage allowance and pack medications in carry-on
  • □ Prepare printed copies of travel documents
  • □ Note: Airline may ask for return or onward ticket
  • □ Have emergency contact information accessible

Visa & Border Entry

60-Day Visa Exemption for US Citizens

You do NOT need to apply for a visa before departure.

US citizens automatically receive a 60-day tourist stamp upon arrival in Thailand at no cost. This is automatic and requires no prior application.

At Immigration (Chiang Mai Airport)

  • □ Have passport ready
  • □ Complete TM.6 arrival card (provided on plane or at airport)
  • □ Show return/onward ticket if asked (can be booking confirmation or future DTV plans)
  • □ Proceed through immigration line
  • □ Receive 60-day tourist stamp in passport

Your 60-Day Stamp Expires: October 24, 2025

This gives you time to complete medical consultations, prepare DTV documents, and optionally extend to 90 days if needed.

First 24-48 Hours in Chiang Mai

Immediately Upon Arrival (Midnight+)

  • □ Get SIM card at airport (AIS, dtac, or True Move booths in terminal)
  • □ Purchase local phone plan or grab-and-go package
  • □ Proceed to accommodation (pre-booked for first 2 weeks)
  • □ Get some rest after long journey

Day 1 - Rest & Basic Setup

  • □ Rest and recover from jet lag
  • □ Download and activate Grab app for local transportation
  • □ Locate nearby convenience store and grocery store
  • □ Set up banking/ATM access (be aware of 220 THB ~$6.50 ATM fees)

Days 2-3 - Essential Setup

  • □ Visit grocery store for essentials
  • □ Identify nearby restaurants and amenities
  • □ Begin research for permanent housing in Suthep area ($250-500/month)
  • □ Start planning first week activities and community connections

Transportation in Chiang Mai

Grab App - Your Primary Transportation

  • Grab Taxi: Similar to Uber, affordable local rates
  • Grab Bike: Motorcycle taxis, very inexpensive for quick trips
  • Download: Available on iOS and Android
  • Cost: Significantly cheaper than Western equivalents

Money & Banking

  • Thai Currency: Thai Baht (THB)
  • ATM Withdrawal Fee: 220 THB (~$6.50) per transaction
  • Tip: Withdraw larger amounts less frequently to minimize fees
  • Exchange Rate: Monitor current rates; ~33-34 THB per 1 USD (approximate)
  • Credit Cards: Widely accepted at larger establishments

Important Notes for First Arrival

  • Medical Records: Keep your US medical records accessible - you'll need them for upcoming medical consultations
  • Medications: Ensure you have your prescriptions in original bottles with labels
  • Recovery Community: 30+ AA meetings per week in Chiang Mai - prioritize connecting early
  • Jet Lag: Give yourself grace during the first week to adjust to the 12-hour time difference
  • 60-Day Timeline: This period is NOT just leisure - use it for medical consultations and DTV preparation

Next Steps

Once you've settled in during the first few days, proceed to the Settling In Guide for your first week planning and establishing essential services.

You'll then move into the Medical Planning phase to begin consultations for your DTV visa application.

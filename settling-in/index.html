Settling In: First 3 Weeks in Chiang Mai

Essential setup for basic services, recovery community integration, and local navigation

Timeline: August 26 - September 16

Aug 26 - Aug 29

First 3 Quiet Days to Settle

  • Go to nearby grocery store
  • Download and set up Grab app for transportation
Info: Grab Taxis & Motorbikes are very inexpensive to travel around Chiang Mai
Aug 26 - Sep 16

Set Up Basic Services

  • Hire a cleaner (500-800 THB / $15-24 for 2-3 hours)
  • Find primary care doctor (recommended: Dr. Morgan at Health Care Medical Clinic)
  • Arrange physical therapist
  • Locate nearby pharmacy
Aug 26 - Sep 16

Connect with Recovery Community

  • Attend nearby AA meetings (50+ per week in Chiang Mai)
  • Explore Buddhist recovery/Refuge Recovery meetings
  • Find sponsor
  • Research addiction counseling options (Counselling Thailand, The Cabin alumni network)
Info: Chiang Mai has an active recovery community with many Western-style restaurants and social options

Local Information & Resources

Getting Around

  • Grab App: Taxis and motorbikes, very affordable
  • ATM Fees: 220 THB ($6.50) per withdrawal
  • Taxis & Motorbikes: Inexpensive throughout Chiang Mai

Housing & Costs

  • Suthep Area: $250-500/month
  • Cleaning Services: 500-800 THB ($15-24) for 2-3 hours
  • Medical Consultation: $40-90
  • Physical Therapy: $25-75/session (vs $100+ in US)

Amenities in Suthep Area

  • Convenience stores
  • Grocery stores
  • Restaurants (many Western-style)
  • AA Meetings & recovery support
  • Counseling centers & art therapy
  • Gyms and swimming pools
  • Physical therapists
  • 24/7 English-speaking medical facilities

Key Contacts & Services

Primary Care

Recommended: Dr. Morgan at Health Care Medical Clinic

Schedule early in your stay to establish relationship

Recovery Support

50+ AA meetings per week in Chiang Mai

Buddhist recovery and Refuge Recovery meetings available

Consider: Counselling Thailand, The Cabin alumni network

Expat Community

Join Chiang Mai Expats Facebook groups

Suthep area has large expat community with English-speaking services

What's Next

After completing this 3-week settling-in period, you'll move into:

  • Medical Planning Phase: Schedule consultation with Bangkok Hospital to develop 6+ month medical plan (required for DTV visa)
  • Deeper Settling In: Join community groups (yoga, hiking, art, cooking, etc.), explore volunteering opportunities, and begin learning Thai language
  • DTV Application Preparation: Compile necessary documents and prepare for visa application

Pro Tips for Success

  • Take it slow your first few days - jet lag and adjustment are real
  • Prioritize finding your recovery community early - it's crucial for ongoing support
  • Establish relationship with primary care doctor as foundation for future medical planning
  • Download Grab app immediately for reliable, affordable transportation
  • Connect with expat groups to learn local navigation and resources
  • Many Western-style restaurants available if needed during adjustment period

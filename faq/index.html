Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the DTV visa, medical treatment, recovery community, and practical logistics for living in Thailand.

DTV Visa Questions

What is the DTV (Destination Thailand Visa)?

The DTV is a 5-year multi-entry visa designed for people engaged in specific activities in Thailand, including medical treatment, wellness, remote work, and other pursuits. It allows you to stay for 180 days per entry with the ability to extend or border-run for continued residence.

Do I need a visa to enter Thailand initially?

No. US citizens receive an automatic 60-day visa exemption upon arrival at Thai immigration. You can apply for the DTV visa after arriving and establishing your medical treatment plan.

Where do I apply for the DTV visa?

You must apply at a Thai embassy outside of Thailand. Most applicants apply from Vietnam (Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City embassy) due to high approval rates and convenient processing times. Applications must be submitted online at thaievisa.go.th within 24 hours of arrival at the embassy country.

How long does DTV approval take?

Official timeline: 5 working days

Realistic timeline: 2 days to 2 weeks, depending on embassy workload and document completeness.

What is the DTV application fee?

The non-refundable application fee is $340 USD. This is paid during the online application process.

Can I apply for the DTV from inside Thailand?

No. DTV applications must be submitted from outside Thailand. You must travel to another country (typically Vietnam) to apply at a Thai embassy.

What happens if I'm rejected?

Rejection is rare if documentation is complete and error-free. However, if rejected, you can typically reapply. The $340 fee is non-refundable. Ensure all documents are accurate and complete before submission to minimize rejection risk.

Medical Treatment & Costs

How much does physical therapy cost in Thailand vs. the US?

Physical therapy in Thailand costs approximately $25-75 per session, compared to $100+ per session in the US. This significant cost difference is a valid justification for the DTV medical treatment category.

Are US medications available in Thailand?

Yes. Many US medications are available in Thailand at a fraction of US prices. However, bring a 90-day supply from the US as backup and carry US medical records for reference. Research specific medications before arrival to confirm availability.

Which hospitals in Chiang Mai are recommended?

Bangkok Hospital in Chiang Mai is highly recommended and has a strong track record of providing medical letters for DTV approval. They offer English-speaking doctors and comprehensive services. Dr. Morgan at Health Care Medical Clinic is also recommended for primary care.

What medical records should I bring from the US?

Bring comprehensive medical records including:

  • Surgical records and reports
  • MRI, X-ray, and imaging results
  • Specialist consultation notes
  • Prescription lists
  • Test results and lab work
  • Physical therapy assessments

These are essential for establishing your medical treatment plan with Thai doctors.

Recovery Community & Social Life

Is there an AA/recovery community in Chiang Mai?

Yes. Chiang Mai has 50+ AA meetings per week, Buddhist recovery groups, and Refuge Recovery communities. There is a well-established expat recovery network. Many meetings are conducted in English.

How large is the expat community in Chiang Mai?

Chiang Mai has a large, active expat community concentrated in areas like Suthep and Nimman. There are numerous Facebook groups, meetup organizations, and social activities organized by expats. The community is particularly strong in recovery, arts, sports, and hobbies.

What activities and groups are available?

Available activities include:

  • Yoga and fitness classes
  • Hiking groups
  • Art and creative communities
  • Cooking classes
  • Board game and hobby groups
  • Volunteering opportunities (teaching English, women's organizations)
  • Language exchange groups

Can I volunteer in Thailand?

Yes. Common volunteering opportunities include teaching English, working with women's organizations, environmental projects, and community development. Research organizations before arrival and verify any visa requirements for volunteer work.

Practical Logistics

How much does housing cost in Chiang Mai?

Housing in Chiang Mai ranges from $250-500+ per month depending on location, amenities, and size. The Suthep area (popular with expats) is on the higher end of this range but offers proximity to services and community.

What about transportation in Chiang Mai?

Transportation is affordable and convenient:

  • Grab App: Ride-sharing via motorbike or car, very inexpensive
  • Motorbike Rental: $3-5 per day
  • Songthaews: Shared red taxis following fixed routes, ~20 THB ($0.60) per ride
  • Taxis: More expensive but available

What are ATM fees in Thailand?

ATM fees are typically 220 THB ($6.50 USD) per withdrawal. Consider withdrawing larger amounts less frequently to minimize fees. Some banks offer fee-free ATM networks.

How much does a cleaning service cost?

Cleaning services in Chiang Mai cost 500-800 THB ($15-24 USD) for 2-3 hours of cleaning. This is a reasonable expense for maintaining living space while managing health concerns.

Are there western-style restaurants in Chiang Mai?

Yes. Chiang Mai has numerous western-style restaurants, cafes, and markets alongside Thai cuisine. The Nimman and Suthep areas are particularly well-served with diverse dining options suitable for various dietary preferences.

What is the 30-day tourist extension?

The 30-day tourist extension allows you to extend your initial 60-day visa exemption for an additional 30 days (total 90 days), costing 1,900 THB (~$57 USD). Same-day processing at the immigration office. This provides extra time if you need it before applying for the DTV.

DTV Application Requirements

What documents do I need for DTV application?

Required documents include:

  • Completed online application form
  • Passport and passport photos
  • Medical plan and letters from Thai doctors
  • Financial Sponsor information and bank statements
  • Proof of relationship to sponsor (birth certificate)
  • Travel/health insurance documentation
  • Completed TM.86 and TM.87 forms

What is a Financial Sponsor?

A Financial Sponsor is a person (typically a family member or close friend) who commits to supporting you financially in case of emergency. They must:

  • Show bank statements proving 500,000+ THB ($15,000+ USD) in savings for 3+ months
  • Provide a signed sponsorship letter committing to cover emergency expenses (e.g., medical evacuation)
  • Provide proof of relationship (birth certificate if family)

Why is the medical consultation important?

The medical consultation with a Thai doctor establishes the foundation for your DTV approval. The doctor reviews your US medical records and creates a 6+ month treatment plan. This medical letter from the Thai hospital is crucial for visa approval. Bangkok Hospital Chiang Mai has proven success with DTV approvals.

Can I stay in Thailand while my DTV is being processed?

No. You must leave Thailand to apply at a Thai embassy. During the processing period (typically a few days to 2 weeks), you must stay in the country where you applied (e.g., Vietnam). You cannot return to Thailand until you receive your approval and re-enter with the DTV visa.

Vietnam Trip & Border Logistics

Do I need a visa for Vietnam?

Yes. US citizens need a Vietnamese tourist visa. The e-visa costs $25 and can be obtained online. Process it before traveling to Vietnam.

How much does a Vietnam trip cost?

Budget $400-600 total for a 1-week Vietnam stay, including:

  • Flights: $100-200 round-trip from Chiang Mai
  • Accommodation: $30-40 per night ($210-280 for 7 nights)
  • Food and activities: $50-100
  • Vietnamese visa: $25

Which Thai embassy should I apply at?

Both the Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City Thai embassies have high DTV approval rates. Ho Chi Minh City may have shorter processing times. Choose based on flight availability and personal preference. Hanoi is more affordable for accommodation.

What happens after I receive DTV approval?

After approval:

  • You receive the DTV electronically via email
  • Print multiple copies of the approval
  • Fly back to Thailand
  • Show the printed DTV at Thai immigration upon entry
  • You are stamped in for 180 days
  • You can stay for up to 5 years using extensions or border runs

Long-Term Stay

How long can I stay on the DTV?

The DTV visa is valid for 5 years. Each entry allows 180 days. You can:

  • Stay continuously for up to 360 days with one extension (costs ~$300)
  • Do a border run every 180 days for a fresh entry
  • Repeat this cycle for the full 5-year validity of the visa

What is a border run?

A border run is leaving Thailand and immediately re-entering to reset your visa stamp and the 180-day clock. Common border run destinations from Chiang Mai include Laos (Vientiane) and Myanmar. This is a standard way to extend your stay without formal extensions.

Do I need re-entry permits?

No. The DTV does not require re-entry permits for 5 years. You can leave and re-enter Thailand freely without needing to file additional paperwork.

What about the burning season in Thailand?

Northern Thailand experiences poor air quality from February-May due to agricultural burning. Many people travel south to beaches or to nearby countries (Bali, Malaysia) during the worst months. Plan ahead if air quality is a health concern for you.
